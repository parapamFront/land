Are online phone numbers “real” telephone
numbers though?
This is an understandable question, as oftentimes people like to be able to physically hold their media, and for many, their cell phone equals their telephone number. Rest assured, online numbers are just as real as any mobile or landline number
Do I need any hardware to use with a small business phone system from MightyCall?

What if I decide to change VoIP providers?
Will I lose my number?
This is a common worry; small businesses who have for years built their brand on a single number could be thrown for a loop if suddenly that was taken away. Rest assured, it isn’t a problem; some companies will port your numbers for free, some for a nominal fee. But nonetheless you can be sure that you can port your number, taking it with you wherever you go, both metaphorically and literally.
What is VoIP?
VoIP stands for Voice over Internet Protocol, and is the technology that allows phone calls and other information to be transmitted through the internet. This allows for much more flexibility and often, efficiency, in communications.
So can I only use online numbers on my computer, on a website?
You can use online numbers on a website, yes, but, if your telephony company offers it, you can also use them on your cell phone/cell phone app! Some also allow you to use softphone software, which is essentially a telephone program on your computer. Using anything on your computer will of course require having access to a microphone and speakers/listening system
Can I get a toll free phone number with a small business phone system?
Yes! MightyCall carries tons of toll free virtual phone numbers- it's just as easy to get one as it is to get any virtual phone number (which is to say- very easy!)
What if I want to buy an online number with an area code that doesn’t correspond to my local area?
This is no issue whatsoever. In fact, many businesses buy online numbers which have area codes of localities other than where they are located. This allows them to be more accepted into a community and to not seem like some far off, alien company coming into someone’s small town.
Can I have multiple virtual phone numbers? with my small business phone system?

I’m old-school. I like the flexibility of online numbers but I want to still use my corded office phone. Can I do so?
You sure can! It’ll take some setting up, but you can set up your online number to work with corded office phones.
